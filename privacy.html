8. In-app purchases, verification and restore

8.1 In-app purchases via Apple/Google

In-app purchases are processed by Apple or Google. We typically do not receive payment data (e.g., credit card data), but rather transaction/purchase information required to unlock or restore purchases.

Processed data may include:

  • product ID (e.g., purchased item),
  • transaction ID / purchase token,
  • purchase date/status,
  • platform (iOS/Android),
  • purchase receipt (if needed for verification).

Legal basis: contract performance (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR).

8.2 Server-side verification / restore server (linking user and purchase)

To verify purchases and enable restore, we may send transaction information to our backend server and store/process it there.

Depending on implementation, we may use a pseudonymous user identifier, for example:

  • a restore/user ID generated in the app, or
  • another technical identifier required to map entitlements to a user.

Purposes:

  • purchase verification (abuse/fraud prevention),
  • entitlement management and unlocking content,
  • restore after reinstall or device change,
  • support for purchase issues.

Legal bases:
- contract performance (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR),
- legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) in security, fraud prevention, and technical traceability.
